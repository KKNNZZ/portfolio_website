Impact of Physical Barriers on Social Interactions in US Cities using ML


The aim of this research project was to analyze to what extend physical barriers, in the form of rivers, railways and highways, limit social interactions. Modularity based machine learning algorithms were used to determine communities within US cities.

Dataset used is from a discontinued social media app called Gowalla. Data includes geographical check-in location and friend connections between users.

Learning experience: Choosing a fitting machine learning algorithm given the goal. Using different data analytics methods to get a better understanding of the many metrics computed. Setting up a project proposal plan and executing it successfully.

Movement network of Oklahoma with weighted edges showing the connectivity between the neighborhoods.

Community detection and labelling of neighborhoods in Oklahoma based on modularity based machine learning algorithm.

Physical barriers in the frorm of rivers, railways and highways splitting cities into subareas. Figure on the right highlightes the average distance to the city center.

Correlation matrix between different metrics and algorithms.
